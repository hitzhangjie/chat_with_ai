"Vibe coding"—relying heavily on AI to generate code through prompts without deep technical oversight—is considered by many software engineering experts to be a "Dunning-Kruger-fueled fantasy" for complex systems. While effective for rapid prototyping, internal tools, or simple MVPs (Minimum Viable Products), the approach frequently fails when applied to long-term, production-grade software. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]  
Here is why "vibe coding" will fail in most professional contexts: 
1. Inability to Handle Complexity and Edge Cases [1]  
Vibe coding is excellent at creating the top-level "look" of an app but fails at the 99% of work that happens under the hood. 

• Edge Cases: AI excels at the expected flow but fails at the infinite edge cases that real users generate, leading to fragile applications. 
• Architectural Failure: Without a human architect designing the system, AI-generated code often lacks logical structure, leading to "spaghetti code" that is impossible to extend. 
• Hidden Business Logic: AI often embeds complex business rules into simple helper functions without proper documentation, making future changes impossible. [1, 6, 7, 8, 9]  

2. High Technical Debt and Maintenance Nightmares 
Vibe coding often prioritizes making the code run now over making it maintainable later. 

• "Garbage" Output: Vibe coding produces code that works temporarily but is often bloated, insecure, and unmaintainable. 
• Fragile Solutions: Instead of solving problems, AI often "patches" them, creating a house of cards that collapses when new features are added. 
• The Debugging Loop: Fixing one AI-generated bug often introduces two new ones, leading to a frustrating, endless cycle of error-fixing. [2, 6, 7, 13, 14]  

3. Lack of Security and Compliance 

• Security Vulnerabilities: AI often skips crucial security measures, such as input sanitization or proper authentication. 
• Data Compliance Risks: Vibe-coded applications may violate data protection laws (like GDPR) because the AI does not understand compliance regulations. [15, 16, 17, 18, 19]  

4. The "Premature Optimization" Trap 
Vibe coding works well when it feels like "magic" for quick tasks, but it fails when the app needs to scale, handle high user volume, or integrate with complex systems. Rebuilding a vibe-coded foundation in the middle of growth is a significant business risk. [20]  
5. It Surfaces Pain, It Doesn't Solve It 
Vibe coding reminds us that programming is hard, but it doesn't eliminate the underlying challenges of software engineering: defining the solution and handling complexity. The skill is not in writing code (which AI can do), but in understanding what code to write and why, which require human oversight. [21]
